Introduction

        We are excited to announce the 2024 5th International Conference on Automous Vehicles, slated to take place fromJune 24 to 26 in Beijing. This conference serves as a pivotal platform for scholars, engineers, practitioners, and policymakers to convene and explore the forefront of intelligent vehicle technology.

        Under the theme, "The Comprehensive Challenge of the Autonomous Driving Era: People, Vehicles, and Society," this conference delves into the multifaceted aspects of the autonomous driving revolution. It addresses the intricate dynamics between human interaction, vehicle technology, and societal impact, encapsulating the diverse challenges and opportunities shaping the future of transportation.

        The conference features an array of compelling sub-themes, including Human Interaction and Factors, Communications, Privacy, and Safety, Motion Planning and Perception, as well as Policy, Ethics, and Regulations. These sub-themes encompass critical dimensions of intelligent vehicle systems, spanning user experience, safety considerations, technical innovations, and regulatory frameworks.

        We extend a warm invitation to researchers and practitioners across a spectrum of disciplines, including artificial intelligence, computer science, electrical engineering, transportation, psychology, sociology, law, and ethics. We welcome contributions in various formats, such as original research papers, case studies, demonstrations, and policy proposals, aimed at advancing the understanding and application of intelligent vehicle technologies.

        Join us at the 2024 5th International Conference on Automous Vehicles to engage in stimulating discussions, foster interdisciplinary collaborations, and shape the trajectory of mobility in the autonomous driving era. Together, let us navigate the challenges and opportunities of intelligent vehicles towards a safer, more efficient, and sustainable transportation landscape. We eagerly anticipate your participation in this groundbreaking event!
